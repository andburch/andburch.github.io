Why is your logo a weird-looking ant?

This baby is an illustration of "Ectatomma metallicum," a misnamed ant featured in W.W. Froggatt's 1907 Australian Insects. (This is the same unfortunately named Walter Wilson Fraggat known for publishing other such popular works as Sheep-maggot flies and their parasites and Insects living in figs, with some account of caprification.) Ah, anyway, the species is now properly known as Rhytidoponera metallica or by its logically consistent common names: the green-head ant / the metallic pony ant. Although I am not yet only recently personally acquainted with R. metallica, I have always been a huge fan of its New World cousin, Ectatomma ruidum. But since ole Froggie passed away a while back and his illustrations are in now the public domain... I figured I and can snatch this one up for free, and most people can't tell a difference anyway!
